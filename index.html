Five Great Hiking Spots
in Lancaster, Ohio

by Cheri Marshall
November 1, 2021

1. Alley Park

lancasterparks.com/charles-alley-nature-park

Charles Alley Nature Park is the outdoor place to go in the Lancaster area! This beautiful park has multiple trails, a lovely lake, a covered bridge, and a nature center with live animals. In addition, Alley Park has summer programs for children and seasonal activities such as the Pumpkin Walk and the Spring Flower Hike. Others can visit Alley Park to kayak, attend birdwatching sessions, or take a brisk walk with the dog.


One of the walking trails at Alley Park, which wraps around the lake.

The log cabin at Alley Park is a popular spot for homecoming dance pictures.

2. Rising Park

naturalohioadventures.com/rising-park-ohio

The most well-known park in Lancaster, Rising Park is home to Mount Pleasant, a large 250-foot bluff that offers a great view of the Downtown Lancaster area. The hike to the top of Mount Pleasant is sure to get your blood flowing, but the view is well worth the climb! Rising is a great spot with a large pond, a covered gazebo, and playgrounds for both toddlers and older children. Rising Park also has several shelter houses that are often used for parties, in addition to tennis courts and some great sledding hills.


A great spot to hang a hammock, these two trees offer a perfect view of the pond and fountain.

The playground for older children at Rising Park, complete with a large rock for climbing.

3. Shallenberger State Nature Preserve

visitfairfieldcounty.org/listing/shallenberger-nature-preserve

With 1.8 miles of hiking trails, you'll be sure to put in some steps at Shallenberger State Nature Preserve. The park has great views from Allen Knob, a promontory of sandstone that reaches 240 feet in height. Breathtaking scenery from the Lancaster countryside is visible on Allen Knob. One of the quieter parks in Lancaster, Shallenberger is a fantastic place to reconnect with Mother Nature.


A rock formation on Allen Knob that allows an excellent view past the trees.

One of several hiking paths at the Shallenberger Nature Preserve

4. Christmas Rocks State Nature Preserve

trekohio.com/2012/06/20/christmas-rocks-state-nature-preserve

The most attractive feature of the Christmas Rocks State Nature Preserve is undoubtedly the rock formation known as Jacob's Ladder, which is around 250 feet high. The view of the valley below is serene and picturesque, and almost 5 miles of hiking trails can be explored at Christmas Rocks.


Larger rocks can be seen near the top of the trail, a signal that the trek is almost over.

A gravel path at the entrance to the park will eventually give way to wooded trails.f

5. Keller-Kirn Park

visitfairfieldcounty.org/listing/keller-kirn-park

Keller-Kirn Park is best known for its frisbee golf course (one of the top 10 in the state), which follows one of the trails and connects to the Flat Rocks Overlook at Forest Rose Cemetary. Keller-Kirn has over 76 acres of area, made up of hiking trails and picnic areas. Some of the back trails are very peaceful and border the beautiful country homes of Lancaster.


The entrance to the Flat Rocks trail at Keller-Kirn is beautiful in the fall.

View of the back end of the park, which borders some gorgeous country properties.
